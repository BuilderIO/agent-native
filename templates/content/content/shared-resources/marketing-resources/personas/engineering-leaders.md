# Engineering Leaders (Exec Buyers)

CTOs and VPs of Engineering who need to prove ROI on dev tools while accelerating velocity without proportional headcount growth.

## Common Titles

- Chief Technology Officer (CTO)
- VP of Engineering
- VP of Technology
- Director of Engineering
- Head of Engineering

## Recognition Signals

You're talking to this persona when:

- They ask about enterprise security, compliance, or legal requirements
- They mention Board reporting, CFO approval, or proving ROI
- They want customer references at similar scale before committing
- They defer technical validation to their engineering team

## Priorities

- **Prove clear ROI on development tools** - Must defend every significant tool investment to CFO and Board with data
- **Accelerate portfolio-wide velocity** - CEO and Board expect faster shipping without sacrificing quality or adding headcount
- **De-risk tool adoption** - Failed rollouts waste budget and erode team trust, making future changes harder

## Metrics They're Measured On

- Engineering velocity and time-to-market
- Quality/bug rates
- Team retention and ROI on tooling investments

## Pain Points

- **Tool evaluation fatigue.** Months testing tools that fail at enterprise scale because generic AI tools don't meet design system or compliance standards.
- **Uncertain compliance at scale** - Tools that work in demos fail security audits or accessibility requirements when rolled out
- **Unpredictable ROI** - Can't justify spend to CFO when setup complexity isn't known until deep into implementation
- **Generic code requires rewrite** - AI tools generate generic code that doesn't match their design systems
- **Frontend capacity constraints.** Frontend becomes the bottleneck because engineers stuck on UI work.

## Why Builder Resonates

- **Get more done with less engineering capacity.** Engineers focus on architecture, performance, and code review. Product and design handle QA and design redlines.
- **Offload tasks off engineering entirely.** Design and PM requests skip engineering execution. Engineers spend time on architecture and complex problems instead of UI implementation.
- **Non-engineers validate and refine work.** Product, design, and QA teams collaborate on real production code. Engineers receive fully verified PRs ready to merge.

## Discovery Questions

### Open (get them talking)

- "What's the biggest bottleneck slowing down your product roadmap right now?"
- "What tools have you evaluated for design-to-engineering recently, and what happened?"
- "Walk me through your current design-to-code workflow - where does it break down?"
- "What's your executive team saying about product delivery speed right now?"
- "How are you currently managing with resource constraints - attrition, backfills, doing more with less?"

_Listen for:_

- **Executive pressure:** CPO/CTO mandates to accelerate delivery, do more with less language
- **Failed attempts:** Recent tool evaluations that didn't work out
- **Resource constraints:** Low backfill ratios or hiring freezes, frontend as bottleneck
- **Process problems:** Slow waterfall handoff processes, low designer satisfaction

### Probe for metrics (funnel toward numbers)

- "What's your typical timeline from design complete to feature in production?"
- "What percentage of your frontend capacity goes to UI implementation vs. business logic?"
- "If you could free up significant frontend engineering time, what would that mean for your roadmap?"
- "How many product teams are you supporting with your current engineering headcount?"

_Listen for:_

- **Metrics:** Timelines in months not weeks, significant capacity on UI work
- **Scale indicators:** Supporting many product teams, resource ratios showing understaffing
- **Business impact:** Ability to quantify business impact of delays

### Validate urgency (understand timeline and drivers)

- "What's driving the urgency to solve this now - is there an executive mandate, board pressure, or competitive threat?"
- "What happens if you don't solve this in the coming months?"
- "Who else is pushing for a solution - your CPO, CTO, Board?"
- "What's your timeline for evaluating solutions and making a decision?"

_Listen for:_

- **Urgency drivers:** Named executives pushing for change (CPO, CTO, Board), aggressive adoption timelines
- **External pressure:** Competitive threats from faster-moving companies
- **Budget signals:** Already allocated vs. still pending
- **Stakes:** Clear consequences of inaction (market position, team attrition, missed revenue)

## Common Objections

**"We tried design-to-engineering tools before and they didn't work"**
Past tools generated generic code outside your codebase. Builder operates directly in your existing repo using your real components. Let your engineers validate the difference.

**"Our setup is too complex"**
Builder works inside existing repositories, design systems, and workflows. We'll run a proof-of-concept with your actual codebase.

**"How do we prove ROI?"**
We provide an ROI model tied to your velocity metrics. Reference customers report significant reduction in UI implementation time.

## Works With

- **Champions** for technical validation. They won't commit without engineer sign-off.
- **Influencers** who push for velocity solutions and create urgency

## Don't Say

- Don't oversell with "it just works." They've heard that before.
- Don't push for commitment before Champions have validated
- Don't dismiss their complexity concerns as edge cases
