# Influencers (Product/Design Leaders)

Product and design leaders under CEO/Board pressure to ship faster, caught between creative quality demands and engineering capacity constraints.

## Common Titles

- Chief Product Officer (CPO)
- VP of Product / VP of Design
- Head of Product / Head of Design
- Director of Product / Director of Design
- Design Manager

## Recognition Signals

You're talking to this persona when:

- They talk about CEO/Board pressure to ship faster
- They frame problems in terms of cross-functional friction or collaboration
- They're measured on velocity metrics they report up to leadership
- They mention wanting to "prove ROI" on design or product investments

## Priorities

- **Accelerate time-to-market.** CEO expects faster shipping. Board measures product velocity as key growth driver.
- **Prove design and product ROI.** Must demonstrate product/design investments drive business outcomes (revenue, retention, conversion).
- **Enable cross-functional collaboration.** Evaluated on how well product, design, and engineering work together.

## Metrics They're Measured On

- Time-to-market / product velocity
- Product adoption and retention
- Design consistency across products

## Pain Points

- **Validated ideas still slow.** Ideas sit in engineering queues for months even after product validation.
- **Building before validating.** Engineering effort invested before user validation. Major bets fail post-launch.
- **Design-to-shipped deviation.** What designers create and what ships are different, causing rework.
- **Prototype-to-production gap.** Prototypes approved quickly, but engineering says "months to build."
- **Senior talent stuck on pixels.** Expensive designers and PMs spend time on redlines. Strategy work gets deprioritized.

## Why Builder Resonates

- **Accelerate time-to-market.** Validated ideas ship faster. Product and design teams prototype in production to test with real users before full engineering build.
- **Non-technical teams move work forward.** Product and design refine real production code themselves. Engineers review and merge.
- **Shift cross-functional dynamics.** Engineers focus on architecture and high-value work. Redlines and design QA go to product and design teams.

## Discovery Questions

### Open (get them talking)

- "What's the biggest friction point between product/design and engineering right now?"
- "Tell me about the last feature that took way longer than expected to ship - what slowed it down?"
- "Where are validated ideas getting stuck before they reach production?"
- "What tools have you tried to accelerate product velocity, and what happened?"

_Listen for:_

- **Executive pressure:** CEO/Board mandates to ship faster
- **Team friction:** Cross-functional friction between design/product/engineering, engineering as bottleneck
- **Capacity waste:** Expensive talent stuck on execution details or redlines instead of strategic work, validated ideas sitting in backlogs
- **Process problems:** PRD bloat or heavyweight documentation processes

### Probe for metrics (funnel toward numbers)

- "What's your typical timeline from validated idea to something in production?"
- "How many features or improvements are sitting in the backlog waiting on frontend capacity?"
- "What percentage of your designers' or PMs' time goes to redlines and implementation details vs. discovery and strategy?"
- "How often does what ships match what was designed - and how many iteration cycles does that typically take?"

_Listen for:_

- **Metrics:** Timelines in months not weeks, designers/PMs spending significant time on implementation details or redlines
- **Waste:** Significant backlogs of validated work waiting on engineering, prototypes getting thrown out and rebuilt from scratch
- **Quality issues:** Multiple iteration cycles between design and engineering, deviation between designed and shipped product

### Map stakeholders (discover who else is involved)

- "Who on the engineering side would need to validate any solution you bring in?"
- "Walk me through the buying process - who has budget authority, who needs to sign off?"
- "How does your CTO or VP of Engineering typically evaluate new tools?"
- "Who else is frustrated by this problem and would champion a solution internally?"

_Listen for:_

- **Key players:** Champions who control technical validation (Distinguished Engineers, tech leads), exec buyers with budget (VPs, Directors)
- **Pain breadth:** Cross-functional pain (not just design/product)
- **Buying complexity:** Clear process with governance/legal/privacy gates, vendor consolidation constraints
- **Change needs:** Executive sponsorship or change management support required

## Common Objections

**"Our engineers won't adopt another tool"**
Builder fits into existing Git workflows and review processes. Involve your frontend engineers early. They validate the output and become advocates.

**"We don't have time for rollout disruption"**
Start with a small pilot on real work. Prove velocity gains before scaling.

**"How do we prove ROI to the board?"**
We provide a velocity metrics framework tied to your time-to-market goals. Reference customers report significant reduction in UI implementation time.

## Works With

- **Exec Buyers** as budget partners on velocity initiatives
- **Champions** who must validate and adopt for any tool to succeed
- **Core Contributors** on their teams who surface workflow pain

## Don't Say

- Don't promise velocity gains without acknowledging engineering adoption risk
- Don't assume they can mandate adoption. Champions can veto.
- Pitch business outcomes, not design features.
